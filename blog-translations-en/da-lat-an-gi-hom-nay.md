# What to eat in Da Lat today?




![What to eat in Da Lat today?]]

Photo: What to eat in Da Lat today?







## 🏔️ 1. Introduction to Da Lat cuisine

Da Lat not only attracts tourists by its cold climate and poetic scenery but also by its unique cuisine, bearing the mark of the highlands. The intersection of traditional and modern culinary culture creates unique flavors that are hard to forget for anyone who has ever enjoyed it.

Under the cold weather all year round, gathering around hot dishes in the peaceful space of the city not only helps dispel the cold but also brings a feeling of warmth and closeness. The cuisine here is not simply food but also reflects the slow, simple pace of life of Da Lat people. From bustling markets to small roadside eateries, each street corner contains its own unique characteristics, contributing to the attractive culinary identity of the city of flowers.

## 🍞 2. Breakfast dishes in Da Lat

### 🥖 2.1. Shumai bread

![Dalat meatball bread]]

Photo: Dalat meatball bread

Shumai bread is one of the popular breakfast dishes in Da Lat. Sushi is made from pureed pork, richly seasoned and served with hot dipping sauce and crispy bread.

### 🥞 2.2. Banh Can Da Lat

![Can Da Lat cake]]

Photo: Da Lat cake can

Banh can is a famous snack but also very suitable for breakfast. Small cakes are poured into terracotta molds, served with sweet and sour fish sauce, shumai and onion fat.

### 🍜 2.3. Hue beef noodle soup

![Dalat beef mud]]

Photo: Da Lat beef mud

Although it is an original Hue dish, beef noodle soup in Da Lat has its own flavor thanks to its special cooking method, rich broth, and soft, fragrant beef.

## 🍛 3. Lunch in Da Lat

### 🍗 3.1. Chicken rice

![chicken rice fried with dalat fat]]

Photo: chicken rice fried with dalat fat

Chicken rice is a perfect choice for lunch, with soft boiled chicken, fragrant rice and rich dipping sauce.

### 🍽️ 3.2. Moist cake with chicken intestines

![Da Lat chicken intestine wet cake]]

Photo: Da Lat chicken wet cake

Chicken intestine wet cake is a typical dish of Da Lat, with a unique combination of soft and smooth wet cake, crispy chicken intestine and sweet and sour fish sauce.
