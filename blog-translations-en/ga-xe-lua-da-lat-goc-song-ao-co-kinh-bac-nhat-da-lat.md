**Da Lat Train Station** is one of the most beautiful ancient stations in Indochina, imbued with French architecture and closely associated with the development history of the city of flowers. With its characteristic triangular roof design, rare cog rails and ancient train carriages, this place is not only a work of historical value but also a popular tourist attraction.

## 1. Overview of Da Lat train station

### 1.1. Address and how to get to Da Lat train station

**Da Lat train station** is located at 1 Quang Trung street, ward 10, Da Lat city, Lam Dong province - just a few minutes from the city center. Thanks to its convenient location, you can easily get here by motorbike, private car or taxi. If coming from Da Lat market, cross Ong Dao bridge, continue along Tran Quoc Toan street towards Lam Vien square. Then turn onto Yersin Street, pass Nguyen Trai, then go straight to Quang Trung and you will reach your destination.

### 1.2. Operating hours and tour ticket prices

Da Lat Station is open to welcome passengers from **7:30 am to 5:00 pm** daily. Visitors can freely visit, take photos, and explore the station's ancient architecture with an entrance ticket of only **50,000 VND/person**. Note, ticket prices and time frames may change from time to time, you should contact the management board in advance to get the most accurate information.

### 1.3. Is Da Lat Station still operating?

Although no longer part of the national railway network, **Da Lat train station** still operates a short tourist train line from the city center to **Trai Mat station**, about 7km away. The journey takes about 25 minutes, suitable for travelers who want to enjoy the slow pace and admire the romantic suburban scenery with pine forests, flower gardens and vegetable greenhouses on both sides of the road.

The final destination of the route is **[Linh Phuoc Pagoda] (Ve Chai Pagoda)** - a famous pagoda with unique architecture made up of millions of pieces of bottles. Ticket prices for this route range from **88,000 - 98,000 VND/trip**, with incentives for round-trip tickets and group travel.

## 2. When was Da Lat train station built?

**Da Lat train station** was started construction in **1932** and completed in **1938**, as part of the 84km long **Thap Cham - Da Lat** railway project, connecting the Lam Vien plateau with the coastal area of ​​Phan Rang (Ninh Thuan). This railway was initiated in **1908** under the Governor General of Indochina Paul Doumer, and stands out with its complex terrain when the entire route's altitude varies by up to 1,500m.

The project is considered one of the rare railways in the world, with **12 stations**, **5 mountain tunnels**, and **16km of specialized cog rails** - technology at that time only available in **Switzerland** and **Vietnam**. Thanks to that, the train line can overcome slopes of up to 12% - an impressive number compared to conventional railway standards.

However, by **1972**, the route was devastated by war and although it was temporarily restored after 1975, it quickly stopped operating due to economic ineffectiveness. Most of the rail system and related works have been dismantled, leaving only **Da Lat station** as a living testament to the golden age of the Indochina railway industry.

Currently, the station no longer serves intercity trains but has been converted into a **famous tourist attraction**, where ancient architecture and historical markers are preserved. In **2001**, this work was ranked as a **National Historical-Cultural Monument**, becoming the pride of the foggy city.

## 3. Explore the unique architecture of Da Lat's ancient train station

**Da Lat Train Station** is a unique architectural work designed by two French architects, **Moncet** and **Revéron**. The highlight of the station is the delicate combination of Western architectural style and indigenous cultural elements. The entire station layout is built symmetrically, with the central part simulating the image of [**three Langbiang mountain peaks**] - the famous symbol of the highlands. The pointed roof is inspired by **Central Highlands communal houses**, creating a harmonious whole, bearing the mark of the region.

Right in the front of the central block is a large clock face - a special detail marking **the moment Dr. Yersin discovered Da Lat**. Ahead are two separate halls: one for passengers waiting for the train, the other for the cargo area. The interior space is simple but scientific, clearly demonstrating French design thinking of the early 20th century. With a combination of architectural aesthetics and historical value, **Da Lat station** is not only a prominent check-in point but also a unique cultural heritage of the foggy city.
