# Sculpture Tunnel - Architectural masterpiece in the heart of Da Lat

**Possessing creative and elaborate architecture, Sculpture Tunnel is known as one of the most impressive clay sculptures in Vietnam. This is not only an attractive tourist destination but also an extremely hot check-in point in Da Lat that you definitely should not miss.**

Included in the list of prominent Da Lat tourist destinations, the Sculpture Tunnel surprises visitors with works that recreate the city of flowers in a completely new way - all meticulously crafted from clay. This space is an ideal "virtual living paradise" for those who love art, are passionate about exploring and hunting for beautiful photos.

## 1. Where is the Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel address?

**Sculpture Tunnel**, commonly known as **clay tunnel**, is located at Da Lat Star tourist area, Ward 4, Da Lat - on the road leading to Tuyen Lam Lake and the famous Truc Lam Zen Monastery.

Surrounded by green pine forests and majestic mountain landscapes, this is an ideal destination for weekend trips with friends and family.

## 2. The process of forming the amazing Sculpture Tunnel

Sculpture Tunnel was born from the unique idea of Mr. Trinh Ba Dung in 2010. Although it only started with a length of only 2km, up to now the project has spent more than 200 billion VND in investment, becoming a symbol of clay sculpture art in Vietnam.

To create highly durable clay blocks in the cold climate of Da Lat, Mr. Dung spent more than 4 years researching to create a soil formula that is resistant to rain, sun, wind and frost - helping the works last a long time over time.

## 3. What is special about the design of Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel?

The journey to create **Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel** is not simply a work of art, but also an inspiring clay story about the history and future of the foggy city. The project is divided into two distinct design phases, each part has a separate theme to recreate the beauty and development of Da Lat through each period.

### 3.1. Phase 1: Recreating the history of Da Lat's formation

The first part of the **Da Lat clay tunnel** takes visitors back to the beginning, where wild nature and pristine mountains and forests still reign. From the time when there were no human footprints until 1893 - the milestone when Dr. Yersin set foot on the Lam Vien plateau - every detail is vividly depicted through clay statues. Wild animals, forests and waterfalls are all vividly recreated, making visitors feel like they are lost in a unique outdoor clay museum.

Next was the discovery period of Dr. Yersin, when Da Lat began to transform with the appearance of architectural works bearing bold Western impressions such as the ancient train station, Bao Dai palace, Linh Son pagoda, Xuan Huong lake and Da Lat market. Each sculpture is meticulously cared for, showing the transition from wild nature to a clearly planned city.

### 3.2. Phase 2: Da Lat present and future

Entering part two, **Sculpture Tunnel** introduces to visitors the image of **Da Lat today** through famous architectural symbols: Lycée Yersin school, Pasteur Institute, Con Ga church, Palace hotel, or the city center market. Every detail is carved with high authenticity, bringing a close and authentic feeling of a Da Lat that is both classic and elegant.

At the end of the tunnel is a recreation of **Da Lat in the future** - a modern, dynamic city with new projects such as Love Valley, Tuyen Lam Lake, Lien Khuong Airport, and highways. The combination of ancient and modern features creates a harmonious whole, helping visitors feel the sustainable development vision of this famous tourist city.

## 4. Impressive records that the Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel has achieved

Not only impressing with its unique architecture made from clay, **Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel** also makes a strong mark on Vietnam's tourism map with a series of proud records, affirming its position as a most elaborate and creative work of art.

### 4.1. The world's longest clay tunnel

One of the most notable records is that this place is recognized as the **longest underground sculpture in the world**. With a length of up to hundreds of meters, this basalt red soil tunnel has become an unmissable highlight in the list of iconic Da Lat tourist destinations.

### 4.2. Unique clay house in Vietnam

Not only does it have a tunnel, the campus also owns an extremely special house made from unburned basalt red soil - an architectural symbol considered to be the most unique in Vietnam. This house was honored to receive **two national records**:

- **The unburned basalt red soil house has the most unique architectural style in Vietnam**
- **The first red basalt house with a roof embossed on the largest map of Vietnam**

## 5. Interesting experience when exploring Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel

### 5.1. See creative clay sculptures

As soon as you set foot in this special art space, you will be overwhelmed by hundreds of clay sculptures recreated extremely vividly. From the legendary song "**Who goes to the land of peach blossoms**", to famous places such as Xuan Huong Lake, Da Lat Station, Da Lat Market... all are expressed delicately, in detail and with artistic depth.

Every small corner of the campus is a "genuine" check-in background for those who are passionate about photography and want to record frames that bear the mark of Da Lat.

### 5.2. Check-in to Infinity Lake - a symbol of love amidst poetic nature

One of the highlights not to be missed at the Sculpture Tunnel is **Infinity Lake** - a place known as a "fairyland" in the heart of Da Lat. The lake is located in an open space surrounded by pine forests and calm water, creating an irresistible poetic scene.

Standing out in the middle of the lake is the statue of K'Lang and H'Biang, symbolizing a romantic love story on the Lang Biang plateau. This is an extremely hot check-in point sought after by young people and tourists every time they come to the dreamy city.

### 5.3. Transform into a fellow citizen - experience a unique local culture

An interesting activity at **Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel** that you should try is renting **traditional costumes** of ethnic minorities such as Lach, Co Ho, Chil or Sre. Dressed in colorful costumes, you will not only get photos with a highland flavor, but also have the opportunity to learn more about the diverse cultural identities of the ethnic groups living in Da Lat.

This is the ideal experience for those who want to combine travel with authentic exploration of local culture.

### 5.4. Enjoy Da Lat specialties and relax

After sightseeing and taking photos to your heart's content, you can stop at the dining area to enjoy delicious local dishes. From snacks like grilled rice paper, hot soy milk, to cool ice creams or fresh fruit juices - all contribute to completing your journey of discovery.

This is also an opportunity for you to **recharge your energy**, enjoy the cool air typical of Da Lat and get ready to continue your inspiring Da Lat travel journey.

## 6. Directions to the Sculpture Tunnel

### 6.1. Directions to the Sculpture Tunnel:

Moving to the Da Lat Sculpture Tunnel tourist area is not too difficult. Depending on your starting location, you can choose one of the following routes:

**From Da Lat market**
Starting from the Da Lat market area, you follow Nguyen Van Cu street, turn left onto Ba Trieu and then continue straight to Dao Duy Tu. Then turn right onto Nguyen Trung Truc and left to enter Trieu Viet Vuong. At the intersection with Hoa Hong Street, turn right, go about 5.4km and turn onto the pass road and you will reach your destination. The distance is about 12km, takes about 30 minutes to drive.

**From Bao Dai III palace**
From Bao Dai III Palace, you go along Trieu Viet Vuong and continue along Rose Street. When you see the clay restaurant, turn right and go about 2.5km further to reach the tourist area.

**From Prenn Pass**
If you are moving from the direction of Prenn Pass, near the Datanla waterfall area, you will see signs leading to the resorts. Turn onto this road, continue 1km to Tuyen Lam Lake. Here, turn right along the lake to the intersection, then turn left and go about 400m further to see a sign to the Sculpture Tunnel. Continue following the signs for about 6km to arrive.

### 6.4. Some notes to remember when going to the Sculpture Tunnel

To make your tour more complete and safe, you should remember the following:

- Even though the access road is paved, there are still many sharp turns, you need to drive carefully and observe carefully.
- You should choose sports shoes or flat sandals for ease of movement, especially when it rains and the road is quite slippery.
- Always pay attention to children, especially in areas near deep lakes.
- Maintain general hygiene, do not litter indiscriminately and avoid stepping on flowers and grass.
- The Infinity Lake area is very crowded with tourists. When taking photos, you should line up and avoid crowding.
- There is an on-site eatery and restaurant serving local dishes, however the cost is not included in the tour ticket.
