# Chicken Church - Classic beauty in the heart of dreamy Da Lat

Located in the heart of Da Lat, Chicken Church is one of the ancient architectural works and long-standing symbols of the foggy city. With its European beauty and nearly a hundred year history, this place is always on the list of check-in places not to be missed.

If you are looking for a review of Da Lat Chicken Church, ceremony hours, directions and sightseeing experiences, this article will help you most fully.

## 1. Introduction to Chicken Church - A hundred year old symbol in the heart of Da Lat

Chicken Church, officially known as St. Nicola Bari Cathedral, was built in 1931 and completed in 1942.
Here it is:

- The largest church in Da Lat
- One of the typical French architectural works
- Outstanding tourism symbol of the city

The church is located on Tran Phu Street - a central route, very convenient to combine visiting famous places such as:

- Da Lat Market
- Lam Vien Square
- Ho Xuan Huong

## 2. Does Con Chicken Church charge a fee?

## 4. Directions to Con Chicken Church

From the center:

- Depart from Da Lat Market roundabout
- Go across the bridge → Le Dai Hanh street
- Turn right → go another ~300m
- Go uphill → see the church

👉 Very easy to find because:

- Located right in the center
- Outstanding high bell tower

## 5. History of the formation of Chicken Church

The church is associated with the development of Da Lat since the French period.

- 1893: Alexandre Yersin discovered the plateau
- 1917: built the first Catholic facility
- 1931: Construction started on the church
- 1942: completed

👉 This is considered a "historical witness" of the city.

## 6. Special features of Chicken Church

### 6.1. Why is it called "Chicken Church"?

The name comes from: 👉 Chicken statue on top of the bell tower

- 58cm high, 66cm long
- As a symbol: French culture, Saint Peter (in the Bible)

### 6.2. Unique architecture

European Roman style church:

- Length: 65m
- Width: 14m
- Bell tower height: 47m

Highlights:

- Symmetrical cross shape
- Main door faces Langbiang Mountain
- 70 stained glass panels imported from France

👉 Light passes through the glass creating a beautiful effect inside.
