Da Lat Railway Station is one of the most beautiful ancient stations in Indochina, imbued with French architecture and closely associated with the development history of the city of flowers. With its characteristic triangular roof design, rare cog rails and ancient train carriages, this place is not only a work of historical value but also a popular tourist attraction.

Currently, Da Lat Station has become a "virtual living" coordinate that cannot be missed thanks to its nostalgic and artistic space. This is an ideal stop for those who want to learn about the city's past and preserve beautiful moments in their journey to explore Da Lat.

1. Overview of Da Lat train station

1.1. Address and how to get to Da Lat train station

Photo: Dalat train station

Da Lat train station is located at 1 Quang Trung street, ward 10, Da Lat city, Lam Dong province - just a few minutes from the city center. Thanks to its convenient location, you can easily get here by motorbike, private car or taxi. If coming from Da Lat market, cross Ong Dao bridge, continue along Tran Quoc Toan street towards Lam Vien square. Then turn onto Yersin Street, pass Nguyen Trai, then go straight to Quang Trung and you will reach your destination.

1.2. Operating hours and tour ticket prices

Photo: Road to Da Lat train station

Da Lat Station is open to welcome passengers from 7:30 am to 5:00 pm daily. Visitors can freely visit, take photos, and explore the station's ancient architecture with an entrance ticket of only 50,000 VND/person. Note, ticket prices and time frames may change from time to time, you should contact the management board in advance to get the most accurate information.

1.3. Is Da Lat Station still operating?

Photo: Train ticket prices - Da Lat train station

Although no longer part of the national railway network, Da Lat train station still operates a short tourist train line from the city center to Trai Mat station, about 7km away. The journey takes about 25 minutes, suitable for travelers who want to enjoy the slow pace and admire the romantic suburban scenery with pine forests, flower gardens and vegetable greenhouses on both sides of the road.

The final destination of the route is Linh Phuoc Pagoda (Ve Chai Pagoda) - a famous pagoda with unique architecture made up of millions of pieces of bottles. Ticket prices for this route range from 88,000 - 98,000 VND/trip, with incentives for round-trip tickets and group travel.

1.4. Unique features only exist at Da Lat train station

Photo: Dalat train station

Not only has historical significance, Da Lat Station also stands out with many "unique" things:

-

It is the only station in the Central Highlands region.

-

Possessing classic French architecture, it is known as the most beautiful train station in Indochina.

-

With an altitude of about 1,500m, it is the highest station in Vietnam.

-

It is the only station that still has a steam train operating in Vietnam.

2. When was Da Lat train station built?

Da Lat train stationwas started construction in1932and completed in1938, as part of the 84km long Thap Cham - Da Lat railway project, connecting the Lam Vien highland with the coastal area of ​​Phan Rang (Ninh Thuan). This railway was initiated in 1908 under the Governor General of Indochina Paul Doumer, and stands out with its complex terrain when the entire route's altitude varies by up to 1,500m.

The project is considered one of the rare railways in the world, with 12 stations, 5 mountain tunnels, and 16km of specialized cog rails– technology at that time only available in Switzerland and Vietnam. Thanks to that, the train line can overcome slopes of up to 12% - an impressive number compared to conventional railway standards.

However, by 1972, the route was devastated by war and although it was temporarily restored after 1975, it quickly stopped operating due to economic ineffectiveness. Most of the rail system and related works have been dismantled, leaving only Da Lat station as a living testament to the golden age of the Indochina railway industry.

Currently, the station no longer serves intercity trains but has been converted into a famous tourist attraction, where ancient architecture and historical traces are preserved. In 2001, this work was ranked as a National Historical-Cultural Monument, becoming the pride of the foggy city.

3. Explore the unique architecture of Da Lat's ancient train station

Da Lat Train Station is a unique architectural work designed by two French architects, Moncet and Revéron. The highlight of the station is the delicate combination of Western architectural style and indigenous cultural elements. The entire station layout is built symmetrically, with the central part simulating the image of the three peaks of Langbiang mountain - a famous symbol of the highlands. The pointed roof is inspired by Central Highlands communal houses, creating a harmonious whole, bearing the mark of the region.

Right in the front of the central block is a large clock face - a special detail marking the moment Dr. Yersin discovered Da Lat. Ahead are two separate halls: one for passengers waiting for the train, the other for the cargo area. The interior space is simple but scientific, clearly demonstrating French design thinking of the early 20th century. With a combination of architectural aesthetics and historical value, Da Lat Station is not only a prominent check-in point but also a unique cultural heritage of the foggy city.

4. Suggestions for cool "virtual living" corners at Da Lat train station

With ancient European beauty in the heart of dreamy Da Lat, Da Lat train station has long become an "outdoor studio" attracting thousands of tourists to take check-in and wedding photos every year. Below are some super beautiful photo shooting coordinates you should not miss:

4.1. Main gate of Da Lat train station

Photo: Unique Da Lat train station

The area right in front of the gate is an ideal stop to take pictures with the background of the typical peaked roof and a large yard filled with flowers and sunshine. With just a reasonable camera angle, you will immediately have a photo that bears the mark of old Da Lat.

4.2. Ticket purchasing waiting room area at Da Lat train station

Photo: History of Da Lat train station

Few people know that the ticket waiting room inside the station is a very "artistic" background with old glass design, wooden tables and chairs, soft lighting and a nostalgic atmosphere. A perfect choice for those who love vintage and classic style.

4.3. Old wooden train cars at Da Lat train station

Photo: Architecture of Da Lat train station

The antique wooden carriages are still carefully preserved at the station, an indispensable highlight for nostalgic photos. The deep brown color, ancient windows and Da Lat sunlight shining through create a cinematic space.

4.4. Railway track area at Da Lat train station

Photo: Da Lat train station gate

The long railway running between the open natural space, covered with green pine trees and cool breezes, is an ideal place to create photos that are both poetic and personal. Just stand along the train tracks and pose gently, and you'll have a million-like photo.

4.5. Ancient steam locomotive - Unique symbol at Da Lat train station

Photo: Dalat train station waiting room

The steam locomotive displayed at the station is a prominent check-in point, with a bold retro style. This is where young people often choose to create "cool", strong and different photos.

4.6. The space inside an ancient train car at Da Lat train station

Photo: Check-in to the old wooden train car at Da Lat train station

Inside the train cars, the ancient interior is still intact: from wooden seats, glass doors to dark tones that carry the mark of time. This is the perfect "so deep" location for those who want to capture quiet, nostalgic moments in the heart of old Da Lat.

5. Suggested tourist attractions near Da Lat train station that should not be missed

After visiting and "virtual living" at Da Lat ancient train station, you can easily combine exploring the surrounding outstanding tourist destinations. Within just a few kilometers radius, you can fully enjoy the natural beauty, architecture and typical culture of the foggy city.

-

Lam Vien Square* (about 1km from the station)*: Outstanding with symbols of wild sunflowers and giant artichoke buds, the square is where many fun activities, art performances take place and is also a familiar check-in point for tourists of all ages.

-

Xuan Huong Lake* (about 1km from the station)*: As the poetic heart of Da Lat, Xuan Huong Lake provides an ideal relaxing space. You can stroll around the lake, duck, ride a horse-drawn carriage or simply admire the scenery and enjoy the mild weather.

-

Da Lat Night Market* (about 2.5km from the station)*: This is a culinary and night shopping paradise. From grilled rice paper and hot soy milk to souvenirs and fashion, the night market is always vibrant and colorful, suitable for exploring Da Lat at night.

-

Bao Dai Da Lat Palace* (about 3km away)*: Palace I is an ancient villa with classic European style, hidden in the middle of a pine forest. The space here is both solemn and poetic, suitable for learning about history and preserving nostalgic photos.
